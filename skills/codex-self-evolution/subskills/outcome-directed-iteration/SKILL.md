# Outcome-Directed Iteration

Use this subskill only through `codex-self-evolution` for material
self-iteration, architecture optimization, or a complete global iteration.

## Terminal Goal

Improve collaboration among the user, verified local experience, and model
execution so useful work completes reliably with fixed quality, safety, privacy,
and authority floors while avoiding unnecessary user effort, model/context/tool
work, and coordination overhead.

## Required Iteration Contract

Before selecting a change, record the smallest evidence-backed statement for:

1. **Outcome:** the concrete task result or collaboration failure to improve.
2. **Capability:** the observable completion or verification behavior that will
   become more reliable.
3. **Collaboration:** the user decision, owner handoff, write surface, and
   final accountable verifier affected.
4. **Economy:** the resource to conserve (user attention, context/model/tool
   use, time, or external/paid action) and why the quality floor is unchanged.
5. **Safety and evolution:** authority, privacy, rollback, feedback,
   invalidation, and no-regression checks.

Use current project authority, verified experience, indexes, and deterministic
validation as the baseline. Do not invent numeric targets; use observable
evidence proportionate to the change.

## Decision Rule

Choose the smallest existing owner or owner-internal subskill that improves the
contract. A top-level owner change still needs its separate explicit user
authorization. Retain the current structure when the candidate has no
evidence-backed net contribution to at least one required check, or when the
claimed economy relies on skipping verification, security, redaction, rollback,
or user authority.

## Closeout

Report the before/after evidence, checks passed, remaining limits, and the
experience or error-feedback destination. Promote a durable rule only after the
observable outcome is verified; otherwise preserve it as a candidate.
