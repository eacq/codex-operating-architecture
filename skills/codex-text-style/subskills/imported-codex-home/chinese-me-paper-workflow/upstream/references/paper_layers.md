# Paper-Layer Checklist

## Whole Paper

- Title states method/object/result boundary, not a broad field slogan.
- Abstract follows: problem -> method -> key algorithm -> quantitative result -> engineering conclusion.
- Introduction ends by naming the unresolved gap and the paper's exact contribution.
- Method section proceeds in computation order; validation section proceeds in evidence order.
- Conclusion mirrors the abstract and only claims what the paper has verified.

## Section Logic

- Each section starts from why the section is necessary, not from a loose definition.
- A section should answer one question: what limitation is being solved, what method is used, what result proves it.
- Put comparisons after method/result, not before the reader has enough context.
- Avoid sections that only describe software operation unless the software is a validation tool.

## Paragraph Logic

- First sentence gives the local purpose.
- Middle sentences provide mechanism, data, formula, or comparison.
- Last sentence connects to the next step or states what has been established.
- If a paragraph only lists concepts, reorder it into cause -> method -> consequence.

## Evidence Placement

- Put equations immediately after the variables and assumptions they formalize.
- Put figures near the first place where the reader needs the geometry, workflow, or result.
- Put tables where comparison or repeated values are easier to scan than prose.
- Use local literature to calibrate accuracy ranges and format style; mark cross-paper comparisons cautiously when experimental conditions differ.

## Innovation Protection

- Keep the thesis's original contribution visible in abstract, introduction contribution paragraph, method section title, result comparison, and conclusion.
- Do not remove core algorithmic details just to make the paper shorter.
- "Refining" means higher density and clearer logic, not deleting the method backbone.
