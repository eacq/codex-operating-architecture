# Experience Knowledge Subskill Refinement

Experience and knowledge workflows should be refined by owner boundary, not by
adding a new top-level skill for every mode. The parent skill stays the
discovery surface. The subskill owns detailed triggers, inputs, outputs,
handoffs, and validation for a mode that still shares the parent's artifacts and
safety boundary.

This pattern was applied to `codex-experience-capture`,
`codex-knowledge-system`, and `codex-error-feedback`: local experience
iteration, workflow learning, private-to-public conversion, workflow-learning
records, global error inbox routing, and continuous-diagnosis feedback became
owner-internal subskills.

Promote a subskill only when independent evidence shows a separate trigger,
artifact set, maintained knowledge base, and safety boundary.
