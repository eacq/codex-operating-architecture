# Content Production Workflow

`codex-task-execution` owns a formal lightweight content-production subskill
for articles, social series, newsletters, explainers, cover briefs,
infographic or comic briefs, and slide narratives. Its routing, provenance,
authority boundaries, and structural validation are verified; reader-value and
revision benefit remain outcome measures for future real content tasks.

## Flow

1. Record audience, channel, goal, source pointers, protected claims, voice,
   deliverable, and review boundary in a compact content brief.
2. Extract a factual spine and one-sentence value proposition, then make an
   outline before drafting.
3. Draft only the requested content form; do not fabricate evidence, quotes,
   citations, endorsements, or the user's voice.
4. Hand visual needs to [[Visual Format Selection]] and `codex-image-workflow`;
   hand editable Office output to `codex-office-cli`.
5. Review factual support, reader value, structure, accessibility, provenance,
   channel fit, and final rendering before delivery.

## Boundaries

Drafting is not publication authority. Social posting, browser login, uploads,
paid generation, third-party runtimes, copyrighted reuse, and installations
need their own authorization and owning workflow.

The workflow borrows the safe sequencing idea from Baoyu's cover, infographic,
comic, and slide skills, but does not install their Bun runtime, multi-provider
clients, browser automation, cookie support, or publisher controls.
