# Academic Text Style Workflow

## Inputs

- Target passage and immediate argument context.
- Language, discipline, genre, and requested intervention level.
- Optional author-supplied exemplar or current primary venue guidance.

## Steps

1. Choose diagnosis, minimal revision, or a profile before drafting.
2. Record the audience, genre, language convention, evidence posture, and baseline.
3. For a manuscript, diagnose argument/section roles before paragraph and sentence edits.
4. Preserve claims, values, citations, formulas, terminology, conclusion direction, and markup.
5. Improve the smallest expression-level surface that improves clarity, cohesion, precision, concision, or register.
6. Compare the result with the source and flag unresolved ambiguity, overclaiming, or missing evidence.
7. Hand citation support, research interpretation, document editing, and exact layout to their owners; use run-safe insertion and render QA for locked DOCX work.

## Verification

- No meaning-bearing protected field changes without author confirmation.
- Terminology, abbreviations, tense, language convention, and reference pointers remain consistent.
- The delivery separates revised text from unresolved risk.
